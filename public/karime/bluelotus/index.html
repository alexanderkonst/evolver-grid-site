I · The Flower

Flower of the Threshold

Nymphaea caerulea

Before the world, there was water. From the dark water rose a single blue lotus, and from its opening petals came the first light. The Egyptians called him Nefertem, the perfume of the sun.

At dawn the flower opens to Ra. At dusk it closes and returns beneath the surface. The priests watched this and read in it the soul's own passage: death, and the rebirth that follows. They laid the blue lotus in the tombs of their kings, so that they too would open again into the light.

Rise like Nefertem from the blue water lily, to the nostrils of Ra. Book of the Dead · Spell 174
