The child who survived through imagination. The child who told stories.

Every productivity system, business framework, and "get serious about your business" message carries the same whisper: **stop playing. Start performing.**

**Master Belief:**

> "Your inner child IS your genius. The fastest path to mastery is through play, not grind."

Ogi's version, from his life: the lotus grows from the swamp. The crisis-child who had nothing became the medicine man who heals everything — not DESPITE the childhood but BECAUSE of it. The inner child doesn't need healing in order to work. The inner child IS the healer.

**The Myth (first iteration):**

> We are taught that success requires growing up. Get serious. Get organized. Get a team. Build systems. Execute.
>
> That's the lie.
>
> The truth: your greatest genius lives in the part of you that never grew up — the part that plays, imagines, creates without permission, tells stories without strategy.
>
> Your inner child isn't the problem. It's the answer.
>
> The ones who change the world don't grow up. They grow IN — deeper into what made them alive before anyone told them to stop.
>
> The swamp doesn't kill the lotus. The swamp IS the lotus.
