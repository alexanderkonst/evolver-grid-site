## The category

**Find Your Top Talent is semantic identity infrastructure.** Or: human coordination infrastructure. Or: collaborative intelligence systems. Pick the phrase that fits the audience.

We are not:

- A networking platform (networking is downstream)
- A matchmaking app for founders (traps us in a niche)
- A career-positioning tool (traps us in a use case)
- A talent marketplace (traps us in a transaction layer)
- Tinder for [anything] (collapses the category)

We are the substrate that lets all of those things become possible at higher precision. The core mechanic is **self-description quality determines opportunity quality** (Domain 89). Networking, matching, recruiting, and collaboration discovery are all downstream of articulation precision. The upstream lever is the instrument that helps each person describe themselves with precision. AI removes the bottleneck that previously required a human coach.

The distinguishing axis vs. LinkedIn and adjacent platforms: **LinkedIn centralizes visibility. Find Your Top Talent decentralizes meaningfulness.** Different category entirely.

## The three layers

Positioning operates on three vertically stacked layers (per **Domain 90**). The discipline is to sequence them: products before mythology, trust before vision.

### Layer 1 — Universal Core (ontology; never changes)

The truth about what we do at the deepest level, expressed in language general enough to land for every audience and specific enough to be recognizable. These lines are timeless. Use them anywhere.

- **Become legible to the right people.**
- **Map who you are. Discover aligned opportunities.**
- **AI-powered self-knowledge for professional discovery.**
- **Deep profiles for meaningful collaboration.**
- **Better self-understanding. Better collaboration.**
- **Precise self-articulation. High-precision matching. Latent opportunity made visible.**

Working principle: any layer-1 line must be true for a solo founder, a community ecosystem leader, a creator, a recruiter, a researcher, and an accelerator simultaneously. If the line privileges one audience, it has slipped down into Layer 2.

### Layer 2 — Audience Translation (same engine; different doorway per audience)

The layer-1 truth translated into each audience's vocabulary. Same product. Different doorway.

| Audience | Doorway |
|---|---|
| **Founders** | Find collaborators who naturally fit your way of building. |
| **Recruiters / hiring leads** | Understand people beyond résumés and keyword matching. |
| **Communities** | Reveal hidden collaboration opportunities inside your network. |
| **Accelerators / incubators** | Match founders, advisors, and operators with greater precision. |
| **Creators** | Turn your unique perspective into opportunity density. |
| **Professionals** | Discover roles and collaborators aligned with your natural strengths. |
| **Researchers / polymaths** | Find people working at the intersection you're moving toward. |
| **Investors / advisors** | See deal flow filtered by structural alignment, not surface signals. |
| **Ecosystem leaders** | White-label coordination infrastructure for your community. Your brand, your governance, our rails. |
| **DAOs / network states** | Member-level coordination infrastructure. Sovereign data, federated matching. |
| **Schools / training programs** | Career-positioning depth tied to your curriculum. |

When a new audience surfaces, write the doorway in their native language before opening the conversation. The doorway is the only thing the audience hears at first contact.

### Layer 3 — Mythology / Manifesto (worldview; comes AFTER trust)

The deeper thesis Find Your Top Talent sits inside. These belong in the founder essay, the vision pages, the manifesto, the founder letter. They do NOT belong on the homepage or in the first conversation. Apple analogy: products first; *"bicycles for the mind"* later.

Manifesto seeds (held in reserve, deployed when earned):

- **Industrial-age identity collapse.** People performing employability instead of expressing capability. The résumé era ends.
- **AI increases leverage asymmetry.** Those with precise self-knowledge will compound; those without will be commoditized.
- **Self-knowledge becomes economically decisive.** Not as a soft skill. As the load-bearing input to every meaningful match.
- **Alignment outperforms brute-force competition.** Coordination beats domination at the scale that matters.
- **LinkedIn centralizes visibility. We decentralize meaningfulness.** Different category. Different topology. Different planet.
- **The substrate stays small; the network compounds large.** Berners-Lee / W3C, Torvalds / Linux. We are playing that game, not the unicorn game.
- **Self-knowledge is the philosopher's stone.** The hero's journey applied to professional life. Excalibur of King Arthur. The founder path.

## The sequencing discipline

For every new surface (page, conversation, deck, intro):

1. **Open with Layer 2 in the audience's voice.** They recognize themselves immediately.
2. **Demonstrate Layer 1 once trust is forming.** The deeper truth the Layer 2 framing was an expression of.
3. **Reveal Layer 3 only when the counterparty asks for it.** The mythology becomes the explanation of what they have already experienced, not an opening claim.

This applies at every scale: a single conversation, a landing page, a multi-week sales cycle, a multi-year brand arc.

Leading with Layer 3 is the most common failure mode for coordination-infrastructure founders. The mythology is correct. The sequencing is wrong. The counterparty receives the worldview as abstraction and disengages before the trust-building products land.

## The substrate positioning lock (per Domain 86)

The org stays small while the substrate compounds large. Two operating disciplines that protect this:

1. **Refuse the temptation to be the biggest platform on the substrate.** Even when invited. Even when easy. Each customer of the substrate is also a potential competitor to any platform the substrate-org might run; the moment the substrate-org becomes a leading platform, the trust regression starts.
2. **Measure success by adoption count, not org revenue.** Substrate org revenue is a lagging indicator of adoption. Optimizing for org revenue first triggers the capture failure mode (Facebook ↔ Open Graph).

Founder framing that reinforces this: *"I am one of the integrators of this system. I am the central node platform-wise even though I will likely end up being one of the smaller ones."* That is the correct shape, not a tension to resolve.
