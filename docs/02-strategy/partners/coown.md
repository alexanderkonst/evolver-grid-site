## 1. Synthesis (one paragraph)

coOwn is an *"agency-native 'Commons OS' for the agentic age"* — a permissioned digital commons where individuals, communities, and organizations own their digital presence, collaborate in Spaces, and deploy curated AI agents called Brains. Provenance, attribution, and revenue are designed to flow back to contributors instead of being extracted by a central platform. Built on top of Norway's Bolder CMS infrastructure (a decade-old agency-native codebase), Swiss-stewarded, with concrete working primitives: UBDI (foundation data layer + provenance), Cognition (private AI that learns only from your permitted materials), Brain (public-facing AI agents others can prompt within your permissions), and Space (collaborative environments). Distinctive vs. Planetir: **coOwn ships product today** (paid tiers live, governance foundation named, four primitives working) where Planetir is more an ecosystem/movement layer with planned web3 token. Framing tagline: *"You cannot have nature-positive without first having human-positive technology."*

**Commercial topology (fundamentally different):** coOwn is closed-source SaaS commons with paid stakes; FYTT is open-source federated commons with per-community deployment. These serve different go-to-market motions and different audience trust patterns.

## 7. Implications for FYTT

### For the Planetir conversation

Daphne and the Planetir team likely already know coOwn (per Silvana, Planetir partners with people in coOwn's orbit). When articulating FYTT in conversation, *"how is this different from coOwn?"* will come up. Crisp differentiation:

> *coOwn is the SaaS commons (single platform, paid stakes, subscription tiers). FYTT is the open-source federated commons (per-community skin, sovereign-but-interoperable holons, no subscription gatekeeping). Different shapes for different needs. For Planetir's stated 200+ communities by 2027 goal, federated open-source is the architecture that compresses the path.*

### For FYTT's own roadmap (structural mirror)

coOwn is the closest existing reference model for what FYTT could become if it went closed-source SaaS. Worth studying as the architectural mirror — the path FYTT is NOT taking, but in implementing similar primitives at high quality. Specific references:

- **Stakeholder Foundation** model is a precedent for FYTT's eventual Phase-2 governance (per [`commons_treasury_evolution.md`](../commons_treasury_evolution.md))
- **UBDI provenance/attribution layer** is a precedent for FYTT's federated identity work
- **Cognition / Brain split** (private learning AI + public-facing agent) is an architectural pattern FYTT may want to mirror (current FYTT AI OS leans toward the "private" half)
- **Per-tier pricing** ($0 / $20 / $200 + $500 stake) is a reference for FYTT's per-individual monetization architecture once it reaches that layer

### For direct relationship

- Possible **interop standard conversation** down the line: how would a FYTT-built canvas import into a coOwn Space, or vice versa? Federated-canvas standards would benefit both.
- Possible **shared infrastructure conversation**: Bolder underneath coOwn is a real platform; could FYTT skin a Bolder-derived surface for an enterprise customer that needs paid SaaS deployment?
- **Non-fits to flag:** coOwn is not open-source. Their Stakeholder Foundation is single-platform governance, not federated. Direct merger of architectures is unlikely; partnership on shared standards is more plausible.
