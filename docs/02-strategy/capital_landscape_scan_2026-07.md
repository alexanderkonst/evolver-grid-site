# Founder-Specific-Knowledge Capital

## Executive synthesis

The market you asked for is real, but it is fragmented across three distinct capital archetypes rather than one clean asset class. The first archetype is **people-first venture**: vehicles that explicitly say they invest in people before, or at least as much as, companies. The clearest examples are South Park Commons, Entrepreneur First, Human Ventures, Index Ventures, United Ventures, and Antler. The second archetype is **founder-aligned non-dilutive and quasi-equity capital**: revenue-share, growth debt, SEAL-style structures, or equity/dividend hybrids designed to preserve founder control. The clearest examples are Calm Company Fund, TinySeed, Lighter, Decathlon, Founderpath, Braavo, Capchase, Pipe, Clearco, Founders First, and GetVantage. The third archetype is **permanent-capital and holdco buyers**: vehicles that buy founder-led businesses without a forced resale timeline, such as Permanent Equity, Tiny, and SureSwift. These are especially relevant when the ŌĆ£business is the founderŌĆÖs articulated differenceŌĆØ and the founder wants liquidity without submitting to a traditional VC exit script. The strongest direct match to your thesis is not simply ŌĆ£people-first VC.ŌĆØ It is the overlap of three things: **identity-forward founder selection**, **a patient or no-exit-required ownership model**, and **a founder-aligned instrument that does not punish staying private**. ## How I screened the landscape

I screened for two independent signals and then required evidence of actual capital deployment. The first signal was **thesis language**: phrases like ŌĆ£we invest in people,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£humans first,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£what weŌĆÖre most interested in is you and your potential,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£we invest in who you are,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£day zero,ŌĆØ ŌĆ£calm companies,ŌĆØ and similar formulations that treat founder identity, founder-market fit, or ŌĆ£specific knowledgeŌĆØ as the investable asset. The second signal was **instrument design**: capped revenue share, shared earnings, revenue-based financing, non-dilutive advances, redeemable or quasi-equity structures, permanent capital, or holdco acquisition models with no forced exit. I then prioritized candidates that publicly show check sizes or funding ranges, named portfolio/customers, and dated evidence of deployment.
