## 0. Strategy in one line

One free threshold in two sizes (solo Direction Call · group workshop) × threshold-recognition copy in each segment's own register × segment-matched channels tested falsifiably × warm base first. Meter: offers made per week. Flywheel: act → learn → rebuild → act.

## 1. The ladder

- Free: **Direction Call** (45 min, cap 5/week stated honestly) or **group workshop** (~90 min, communities/venues)
- Paid: **$555** Productize Yourself session → **$1,111** BUILT container
- One free step per channel. Webinar attendees go straight to $555, never to a second free step.

## 2. Direction Call (canonical design)

- Result: "You leave with your transition named and your strongest next direction on the table. Practically everyone does."
- Why-free (message #2 after accept): "Why free? Because the first 45 minutes is where my work does the most visible good, and it's how I choose who I go deeper with. If we're a fit, I have paid containers and I'll say so in one sentence, not in a pitch."
- Two-question filter (service framing): (1) What's the transition you're in? (2) What have you tried? — state + motion; answering Q1 starts the work.
- Week 1 manual, week 2 automate what was learned.

## 4. Cold test protocol (per segment)

- Hypothesis pre-registered: "30 touches → ≥20% accept, ≥15% reply, ≥2 Direction Calls in 7 days." Miss = benched.
- Prospect scorecard before sending (send at ≥4/5): threshold signal visible · built something real · top-5 market · active ≤30 days · any warmth.
- Chain logged per person: sent → accepted → replied → booked → showed → paid.
- LinkedIn budget ~100 invites/week, note ≤300 chars.
- Friday: OMTM count, per-segment funnel, segment scores re-ranked on real data (12-axis instrument, section 8).

## 5. Webinar engine (group Direction Call)

- Arc: philosophy briefly → profiles created live on platform → "you now have the words" → AI tightens your LinkedIn → reveal: your professional profile IS your business. Plus live threshold readings (volunteers).
- Host mechanics: leader posts it as a gift from within the community; co-host optional; can preview solo first; Sasha can bring own host (Karime, Victoria).
- Host economics (pitch openly): 33% of each member's FIRST TWO purchases — $183/session, $367/BUILT ≈ ~$300/mo per community at baseline (10 attendees, 10% buy, ⅓ ascend), ~$3,600/yr for one post.
- Falsifiable: ≥15 attendees → ≥10 live profiles → ≥1 paid in 2 weeks. Predictive metric: profiles-created-live.
- Engine 4 = same SOP in physical CDMX entrepreneur spaces, monthly per venue. First hub pitch: tomorrow.
- Sequenced door: host sees revenue share work → community onboarding-module conversation opens (ecosystem holon, value-first).

## 6. Copy laws (all channels)

- Threshold hook in harvested client language (limbo, spinning wheels, boat in waves) → recognition → magnetic story in one line → one CTA.
- Being-wanted register: no chasing, no over-giving. Sell person-holon only; narrate the cathedral, never pitch it.
- No em-dashes, no "Not X. Y." in external copy.

## 7. Passive layer (LinkedIn, finalized Day 123)

- Tagline: shelf-first format (final wording Sasha's pick of A/B/C, Day 123 session).
- Banner: "are you clear on your next professional chapter yet?"
- Architecture: banner asks about them (profile visit = post-curiosity); tagline answers who I am (travels in previews). No identity labels; self-selection by situation.
- About: threshold hook → mechanism → real arcs → credibility stack (MIT Sloan, Google, Bell Labs, UN, ~$1M AT&T, Mexico regulator director, MIT Solve judge) → Direction Call CTA + why-free.
