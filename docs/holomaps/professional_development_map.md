# Integral Professional Development Map

v1.0. Sasha's original. Source CSV in `assets/`.

16 stages, 4 tiers: Employee (1-5), Entrepreneur (6-8), Founder (9-12), Investor/Mentor (13-16).

---

## Employee Stages

### Stage 1: Dependent Contributor

Begins with minimal work experience, relies heavily on direct instructions, and requires close supervision to complete tasks.

- **Characteristics:** Waits for specific instructions; highly reactive. Does not seek clarification when confused. Fails to report task completion proactively.
- **Inner Growth:** Self-Awareness — limited understanding of personal work habits and impact. Mindset — relies heavily on external direction, lacks initiative. Emotional Intelligence — minimal recognition of team dynamics.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Recognizing the need for personal responsibility. Identifying areas for basic skill improvement.
- **Development Goals:** Building foundational discipline and work ethic. Cultivating a proactive mindset.

### Stage 2: Emerging Self-Starter

Starts taking initiative by completing assigned tasks reliably, seeks clarification when needed, and proactively reports completion.

- **Characteristics:** Completes assigned tasks with higher success rates. Proactively reports task completion and seeks new tasks. Asks questions to clarify uncertainties.
- **Inner Growth:** Self-Confidence — begins to trust own abilities. Communication — understands the importance of clear dialogue. Learning Orientation — starts to seek feedback for improvement.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Reflecting on task performance and outcomes. Setting personal improvement goals.
- **Development Goals:** Enhancing communication and interpersonal skills. Developing a habit of continuous learning.

### Stage 3: Proactive Problem Solver

Identifies problems within their scope, suggests solutions, and takes initiative to improve processes with some guidance.

- **Characteristics:** Identifies problems and suggests potential solutions. Takes initiative with minimal supervision. Demonstrates increased productivity.
- **Inner Growth:** Initiative — takes ownership of tasks and seeks efficiencies. Critical Thinking — begins to analyze and improve processes. Self-Motivation — driven by internal goals and standards.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Evaluating problem-solving strategies. Recognizing the impact of personal contributions.
- **Development Goals:** Strengthening analytical and decision-making skills. Fostering innovation and creativity.

### Stage 4: Autonomous Operator

Manages tasks independently, solves problems proactively without supervision, and reports progress periodically, reducing the need for oversight.

- **Characteristics:** Manages tasks independently. Reports progress in batches, reducing need for supervision. Begins to manage small projects or initiatives.
- **Inner Growth:** Accountability — fully owns responsibilities and outcomes. Time Management — efficiently prioritizes and organizes work. Adaptability — adjusts to changing demands seamlessly.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Monitoring personal productivity and effectiveness. Identifying strengths and areas for growth.
- **Development Goals:** Enhancing self-management and organizational skills. Building resilience and adaptability.

### Stage 5: Intrapreneurial Leader

Takes ownership of entire areas of work, innovates within their role to add value, mentors others, and contributes strategically to organizational goals.

- **Characteristics:** Takes ownership of entire areas of work. Innovates to add value and improve processes. Mentors and guides junior employees.
- **Inner Growth:** Leadership Mindset — sees oneself as a leader within the organization. Strategic Thinking — aligns personal goals with organizational objectives. Emotional Intelligence — empathizes and connects deeply with team members.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Reflecting on leadership style and impact. Seeking feedback to enhance team performance.
- **Development Goals:** Developing strategic and visionary thinking. Enhancing coaching and mentoring abilities.

## Entrepreneur Stages

### Stage 6: Aspiring Entrepreneur

Pursues independent projects or side ventures, experiments with ideas, and begins to understand market needs and entrepreneurial principles.

- **Characteristics:** Pursues side projects or business ideas. Seeks knowledge, resources, and networks. Balances experimentation with learning.
- **Inner Growth:** Self-Belief — cultivates confidence to pursue independent ventures. Passion Discovery — identifies personal drivers and motivations. Risk Tolerance — becomes comfortable with uncertainty.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Assessing personal strengths and weaknesses. Understanding market needs relative to personal vision.
- **Development Goals:** Building entrepreneurial knowledge and skills. Developing a clear value proposition.

### Stage 7: Emerging Entrepreneur

Launches a startup or small business, starts generating revenue, builds a customer base, and navigates initial business challenges.

- **Characteristics:** Launches a startup or small business. Begins generating revenue and building a customer base. Faces and navigates initial business hurdles.
- **Inner Growth:** Resilience — learns from failures and persists through challenges. Adaptability — adjusts strategies based on feedback and results. Growth Mindset — embraces continuous learning and improvement.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Evaluating business performance and personal leadership. Identifying opportunities for pivoting or scaling.
- **Development Goals:** Refining products or services based on market feedback. Strengthening business acumen and financial literacy.

### Stage 8: Scaling Entrepreneur

Successfully grows the business, scales operations to meet increasing demand, implements systems for sustainability, and expands the team and market presence.

- **Characteristics:** Successfully scales operations to meet demand. Implements systems for sustainable growth. Expands team and organizational capacity.
- **Inner Growth:** Vision Expansion — broadens the scope of business aspirations. Leadership Development — builds and nurtures a capable team. Ethical Foundation — aligns business practices with personal values.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Assessing leadership effectiveness and company culture. Ensuring alignment between personal and business ethics.
- **Development Goals:** Enhancing strategic planning and execution. Fostering a strong, values-driven organizational culture.

## Founder Stages

### Stage 9: Innovative Founder

Launches a scalable and innovative startup with a strong vision, applies best practices, and focuses on creating significant market impact.

- **Characteristics:** Launches a scalable and innovative startup. Applies best practices while fostering unique approaches. Focuses on creating significant market impact.
- **Inner Growth:** Self-Actualization — pursues a mission aligned with personal purpose. Creative Confidence — trusts in personal ingenuity to drive innovation. Holistic Thinking — considers broader impact on society and industry.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Reflecting on personal mission and its alignment with business goals. Evaluating innovative capabilities and openness to new ideas.
- **Development Goals:** Enhancing visionary leadership. Building a strong, purpose-driven organizational foundation.

### Stage 10: Growth-Oriented Founder

Achieves product-market fit, accelerates growth, manages increasing operational complexity, and focuses on customer acquisition and retention.

- **Characteristics:** Achieves product-market fit and accelerates growth. Manages increasing complexity with agility. Focuses on customer acquisition and retention.
- **Inner Growth:** Strategic Mindset — balances vision with operational demands. Empathy — deepens understanding of customer and employee needs. Integrity — upholds ethical standards amid growth pressures.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Analyzing the effectiveness of growth strategies. Assessing the health of organizational culture and morale.
- **Development Goals:** Strengthening strategic leadership skills. Enhancing stakeholder relationships.

### Stage 11: Visionary Leader

Positions the company as a market or industry leader, drives innovation, shapes market trends, and mentors emerging leaders.

- **Characteristics:** Positions the company as an industry leader. Drives innovation and shapes market trends. Mentors emerging leaders and entrepreneurs.
- **Inner Growth:** Legacy Building — thinks beyond immediate success to long-term impact. Mentorship — invests in developing others within and beyond the organization. Self-Reflection — regularly evaluates personal growth and leadership journey.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Reflecting on personal and organizational legacy. Identifying ways to contribute to industry advancement.
- **Development Goals:** Expanding influence responsibly. Promoting sustainability and social responsibility.

### Stage 12: Serial Founder and Mentor

Repeats entrepreneurial success across multiple ventures, refines effective startup methodologies, invests in or advises other startups, and contributes significantly to the entrepreneurial ecosystem.

- **Characteristics:** Repeats entrepreneurial success across multiple ventures. Embodies and refines effective startup methodologies. Actively invests in or advises other startups.
- **Inner Growth:** Mastery — achieves high levels of expertise and insight. Altruism — prioritizes giving back to the community. Continual Growth — remains open to learning and evolving.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Evaluating the impact of mentorship and investments. Reflecting on personal fulfillment and future aspirations.
- **Development Goals:** Sharing knowledge and fostering innovation in others. Pursuing new challenges to drive personal and industry growth.

## Investor / Mentor Stages

### Stage 13: Strategic Advisor

Begins sharing expertise to guide others, focusing on mentorship and advisory roles.

- **Characteristics:** Provides mentorship and advice to startups and emerging leaders. Joins advisory boards to offer strategic guidance. Shares insights from personal experience to help others navigate challenges.
- **Inner Growth:** Reflective Wisdom — draws upon accumulated knowledge to guide others. Altruism — motivated by a desire to contribute to others' success. Humility — recognizes the limits of one's own knowledge and remains open to learning.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Evaluating the effectiveness of advisory roles. Reflecting on the balance between guiding and allowing others to lead.
- **Development Goals:** Enhancing coaching and mentoring skills. Learning to adapt advice to diverse contexts and personalities.

### Stage 14: Angel Investor

Invests personal resources in early-stage ventures, combining financial support with mentorship.

- **Characteristics:** Invests personal capital into early-stage startups. Offers not just funds but also mentorship and networking opportunities. Selects ventures that align with personal values and vision.
- **Inner Growth:** Stewardship — takes responsibility for nurturing new ventures. Risk Assessment — develops keen insight into evaluating opportunities. Patience — understands the long-term nature of investments and impact.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Assessing investment criteria and decision-making processes. Reflecting on the alignment between investments and personal impact goals.
- **Development Goals:** Improving financial acumen related to venture investing. Building a diversified investment portfolio that reflects personal mission.

### Stage 15: Venture Capitalist / Impact Investor

Manages larger-scale investments aimed at significant impact, often in collaboration with others.

- **Characteristics:** Manages or contributes to investment funds focused on scaling impactful ventures. Influences industry trends through strategic investments. Champions causes such as sustainability, social justice, or technological advancement.
- **Inner Growth:** Global Perspective — seeks to create systemic change through investments. Ethical Leadership — prioritizes ethical considerations in investment decisions. Collaborative Influence — works with other investors to amplify impact.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Evaluating the social and environmental impact of investments. Reflecting on leadership role within the investment community.
- **Development Goals:** Deepening understanding of global issues and how business can address them. Building partnerships to enhance investment impact.

### Stage 16: Legacy Builder and Philanthropist

Focuses on creating a lasting legacy through substantial contributions to societal well-being.

- **Characteristics:** Establishes foundations or initiatives to address societal challenges. Engages in significant philanthropic efforts. Works to inspire systemic change at the societal or global level.
- **Inner Growth:** Transcendence — moves beyond personal success to focus on legacy and lasting impact. Generativity — seeks to empower future generations. Inner Fulfillment — finds meaning in contributing to the greater good.
- **Self-Assessment Focus:** Reflecting on the desired legacy and how current actions contribute to it. Assessing the sustainability and long-term impact of philanthropic efforts.
- **Development Goals:** Strategizing for maximal long-term impact. Ensuring initiatives are set up to continue beyond personal involvement.

## ❤️ Essence

Sixteen stages trace one arc: from waiting for instructions to building a legacy that outlives you. Each stage moves outer behavior (what you do, what you own, who you lead) and inner dimensions together — self-awareness, mindset, emotional intelligence, agency all deepen in lockstep with responsibility. No stage skips the inner work. The employee who learns to speak up is doing the same kind of growth, at smaller scale, as the founder who learns to let go.

## 🧠 Significance

The map turns "what's next for me" from a vague feeling into a question you can answer by reading a row. It locates any professional — or an AI collaborator working alongside one — on a single developmental line, tier and stage both named. That's also why it works as a calibration instrument for AI operating altitude: match the assistant's tone, autonomy, and initiative to the human's actual stage, not a guess.

## 🔥 Consequences

Once a profile carries a stage-location, matchmaking stops being random and becomes developmental. Match people across adjacent stages on purpose: a mentor one tier up, a peer at the same stage, an investor two stages ahead who's been where you're headed. The platform's Proactive Matchmaker can use this directly — propose introductions that serve each person's next stage, not just the ask they typed today.
