## Cell Entries

### 2026-07-07 · Source: Brown, *Dollars Want Me* (1903) → [full study](./sources/henry_harrison_brown_dollars_want_me.md)

**Meaning × Stage 5 (Generative):** The founding inversion of prosperity consciousness: "'Dollars want me!' in place of 'I want dollars!'" Money is delegated power seeking a self-conscious user; the human is the only source of value ("He needs no money for he is value," Emerson). The stage 4→5 transition is precisely the reversal of the desire vector: from hunting to humming. Brown names the stage-3-and-below signature as "want" consciousness, a developmental holdover ("Brutes want; Man has").

**Flow × Stage 5:** "The hoarded Dollar does not work... The Dollar you spend is the only one you really have." Money produces value only in passage ("like water in the stream... passed under the wheel"). Spending done rightly is investment; every outgoing dollar returns "laden" with the thought sent with it.

**Power × Stage 4-5:** "The Dollar stands for INDIVIDUAL LIBERTY," yet mastery is attitudinal, not quantitative: the average rich man is "the veriest slave... to his fears." Success = "personal power which meets every condition without anxiety."

**Price/Value × Stage 5 (the constraint clause):** The Two Poles of the magnet: (a) possess something the world needs and gladly give a dollar's worth per dollar; (b) dedicate every incoming dollar to noble service. Attraction without real supply is wishing. This is Brown's own guard against the spiritual-fluff trap.

**Debt/Trust × Stage 3:** "There can be no liberty to him who feels the slavery of debt." Debt named as felt bondage, i.e. a stage-3 experience to be dissolved by attitude and clearing, not analyzed structurally.

**Cross-reference to corpus:** Brown 1903 = the UL (interior stance) that ITFT builds the UR/LR physics for. Honey-pot (Brown) ≙ "You don't hunt. You hum." (ITFT). Two Poles ≙ ITFT's crystallization (real value under constraint) + STF's perceived-usefulness filter. Brown lacks the Outer Lens entirely; the corpus supplies it. The two bodies of work are complementary halves: Brown gives the *stance*, ITFT gives the *lens*.

### 2026-07-07 · Source: Hotep, *How to Sell Water to a Whale* (2010) → [full study](./sources/hotep_sell_water_to_a_whale.md) · ⚠️ quarantined (partial frequency pass)

**Exchange × Stage 2-3 (Transactional/Accumulative):** The definitive field specimen of battle-frame selling: customer as opponent, sale as won engagement, tricks as weapons. Documents the ceiling of this stage: its own summit insight is "The #1 Rule of Sales is NOT to sell" (aikido, winning without fighting), i.e. even the hustler octave, pushed to mastery, discovers non-selling. Clean extractables: WHY/HOW over facts; 4 Pillars worksheet (who benefits / who serves them / where they shop / what they consume); Service = Sales; lead by example (self-use as testimony); flagship first; make purchase easy; affirmative-expectation questions.

**Price/Value × Stage 2-3:** Perceived value treated as a manipulable variable (SRP cross-out, fake free shipping, forget-to-cancel trials). The corpus position: these work by borrowing against trust; STF's Reciprocal Coherence prices the debt they never account for.

**Cross-stage finding (the map's first vertical result):** Brown (Stage 5, mystic register) and Hotep (Stage 2-3, street register) independently converge on the same apex sentence: *do not sell; be that which is sought.* The truth is stage-invariant; only its costume changes. ITFT holds the integrated version: "You don't sell. You love. The sale is what love produces when it meets infrastructure."
