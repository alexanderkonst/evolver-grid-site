## Frequency Test Verdict: PASS with one boundary clause

The core stance is service: "servant communicator": someone thinking about the person receiving the communication. Anti-overwhelm as care ("if someone was thirsty and you came with a fire hose, you didn't serve them, you hurt them"). "They will thank you for selling them something." Buyers profiting more than sellers, echoed from Golden.

**The boundary clause:** his method is explicitly *feelings-engineering* ("can you create the feeling in a person to want to feel like it? Yes"; "I'm making you believe that this is potentially the easiest way"). This passes the frequency test **only when the belief being installed is true**: possible-easy-trust framing in service of a real capability is teaching; the same mechanics wrapped around a false promise is manipulation. The corpus adopts the mechanics with the truth-clause welded on. (Compare: playbook Step 0.5 register test; ITFT "alignment confirmed, not manipulation.")

Minor caveat: the in-video funnel machinery (challenge ad, DM CTAs, free gear guide) is standard creator-economy plumbing; mild, transparent, and self-aware; not disqualifying.

## The Native Taxonomy

1. **Teaching is the highest-paying skill** (contra "sales is"): the deliberate correction of the sales-first gospel.
2. **The four phases of business** — lead generation → lead conversion → fulfillment → retention/ascension — and the master move: **teach at every phase**. Teach to attract, teach to convert, teach to fulfill, teach to ascend. One skill runs the whole machine; no sales team required.
3. **The #1 mistake: overteaching.** "An overwhelmed person never takes action." Information-maximizing is selfish teaching; the fire hose hurts the thirsty.
4. **The objective of teaching is a feeling, not information transfer:** "the goal of teaching is to create the feeling in a person to take the next step." People do things for one reason: because they feel like it (via Eileen Wilder).
5. **Teach to shift beliefs**, specifically three: *it's possible* · *it's easy* · *I can trust this person.*
6. **Teach what / when / why / where — not how.** How-teaching loses people (overwhelm); what/when/why-teaching moves them. (Title with "how," deliver what/when/why.)
7. **Named intellectual property:** document your subconscious formula, name it, frame it ("the 131 method," "the baby steps"). Golden's $110k gem: build frameworks and formulas across your expertise systematically. Named frameworks make the learner feel smart, and belief-shift sticks.
8. **Context determines value:** the same information is free on YouTube and expensive in a paid room, because payment buys *attention*, and post-transaction attention is a different physiological state. "Where your treasure is, there your heart is."
9. **Case evidence:** Paige: new Instagram account January, 300k followers by teaching, $300 product × 3,000 buyers = $1.2M, no sales team. His own business: ads → teach → next teach → next level of teach.

## Depth Layers

### Art
- The fire hose and the thirsty person: the definitive anti-overwhelm image.
- The unedited video as proof-of-frame: "this video is not edited" as living demonstration that easy-is-possible.
- Jesus teaching in parables as the master pattern: knowing the audience's problem better than they do, speaking in images.

### Wisdom
1. Teaching collapses the sales function into the value function: when the marketing IS a sample of the product (teaching), the funnel and the fulfillment are one substance. (Direct rhyme with ITFT Touchpoints = Offers: "each touchpoint IS the product.")
2. Overwhelm is not generosity; it is a conversion killer AND a disservice simultaneously: the rare case where kindness and conversion point the same direction.
3. The felt-sense trio (possible · easy · trust) is a compact of STF's Believability of Outcome + Cognitive Simplicity + Status/trust factors, rediscovered from the practitioner side.
4. Value is context-dependent, not content-dependent: pricing is partly the design of an attention container.

### Practice
1. Audit every business phase: where can teaching replace what is currently chased? (ads, sales calls, onboarding docs, retention emails)
2. Before any teaching artifact: name the ONE next step and the ONE feeling that produces it; cut everything that doesn't serve that feeling.
3. Convert the subconscious formula into named IP: document what you actually do with clients, name it, teach the name.
4. Title with how, deliver what/when/why; keep the how for the paid container (truth-clause: the free layer must still be genuinely useful).
