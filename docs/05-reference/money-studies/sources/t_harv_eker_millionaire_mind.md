**Source:** T. Harv Eker, *Secrets of the Millionaire Mind: Mastering the Inner Game of Wealth*, HarperBusiness, 2005. #1 NYT bestseller, 5M+ copies. Lineage: New Thought (Brown → Hill → Eker) industrialized through the 1990s-2000s seminar circuit.

## Frequency Test Verdict: PASS with two caveats

1. **Register caveat:** the rich/poor contrast tables carry a contempt-adjacent edge ("poor people do X"). The mechanism is sound; the class language is not. Corpus translation: read "rich/poor" as *stage* language (Money Holomap stages 3-4 vs 1-2), never as class or worth.
2. **Business-model caveat:** the book is the front end of a seminar upsell machine (free Millionaire Mind Intensive → high-pressure five-figure program upsells). Take the teaching, not the monetization pattern. (Same rule as Golden; see [that study](./myron_golden_body_of_work.md) for the rule stated in full.)

## The Native Taxonomy

1. **The Money Blueprint** — the subconscious financial thermostat, set in childhood.
2. **The three conditioning channels** — verbal programming (what you heard) · modeling (what you saw) · specific incidents (what you felt).
3. **The process of manifestation:** T→F→A=R (thoughts → feelings → actions = results). Roots produce fruits; to change the fruits, change the roots.
4. **The 17 Wealth Files** — contrast pairs of stage-3/4 vs stage-1/2 money thinking.
5. **The retraining protocol** — awareness → understanding → disassociation → reconditioning (declarations + physical rituals).

## Depth Layers

### Art
- The **thermostat**: outer results snap back to the inner setting; a room can't stay warmer than the dial. The definitive image for revenue ceilings.
- The **roots and fruits** tree: visible money results are fruits; the blueprint is the root system underground.

### Wisdom
1. "Your income can grow only to the extent that you do." The inner game bounds the outer game.
2. Given identical tactics, different blueprints produce different results; given a winning lottery ticket, a scarcity blueprint returns to baseline (lottery-winner regression as evidence).
3. Load-bearing Wealth Files: commit to being rich vs want to be rich · think both/and vs either/or · focus on opportunities vs obstacles · get paid on results vs time · manage money regardless of amount · act in spite of fear (not absence of fear).
4. Diagnosis is tractable: trace the three channels and the blueprint becomes visible, hence changeable.

### Practice
1. **Blueprint archaeology:** write out what you heard, saw, and felt about money before age ~13; find the sentence still running.
2. **Declarations** with hand on heart ("I have a millionaire mind") — Brown's affirmations with somatic anchoring.
3. **The 6 jars:** 55% necessities · 10% Financial Freedom Account (never spent, only invested) · 10% long-term savings · 10% education · 10% play (must be spent, guilt-free) · 5% giving. Money management as blueprint retraining through ritual, at any income level.
4. **Act in spite of fear:** the practice unit is the uncomfortable action taken while the old blueprint protests.
