## Frequency Test Verdict: HIGHEST PASS of the first four sources, two caveats

The core stance is dignity and service: "selling is a wealthy exchange of value" where the buyer must profit more than the seller. No contempt in the root frame. Caveats:

1. **Theology caveat:** the biblical frame (seed time and harvest, giving as first movement) is load-bearing for him; the corpus imports the *mechanism* (giving initiates flow; offers are seed), not the theology wholesale.
2. **Business-model caveat (the shared rule, stated once for both Eker and Golden):** *teaching ≠ business model.* Both teachers monetize through mass-event funnels with aggressive upsell architecture (Eker: free-seminar → five-figure intensives; Golden: challenge → $30k-$100k+ masterminds with stage-close mechanics). The corpus adopts their frameworks; it does not adopt pressure-close, stacked-scarcity, mass-upsell machinery, which violates funnel monogamy, the being-wanted register (playbook Step 0.5), and the Purity Check. Extract the ore; don't copy the mine.

## The Native Taxonomy

1. **The 4 Levels of Value** (signature instrument) — what you are paid *from*:

| Level | Work | Currency | Typical ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Implementation | Doing the task with your hands | Muscle | Lowest |
| 2. Unification | Managing those who implement | Management | Middle |
| 3. Communication | Moving people with words (sales, teaching, media) | Mouth | Six-seven figures |
| 4. **Imagination** | Creating what did not exist (offers, systems, IP, ventures) | Mind | Unbounded |

You are paid at the level you work from, not the effort you spend. Effort is maximal at level 1 and pay is minimal; the inversion is the whole lesson.

2. **Make More Offers** — the highest-leverage revenue behavior. Businesses starve from offer scarcity (too few, too timid, too cheap), not product weakness. Revenue is a lagging indicator of offer emission rate.
3. **Premium pricing as service** — charging more forces a better result, selects committed clients, funds excellence. "Rich people buy value; poor people buy price" (translate to stage language, per the Eker rule).
4. **Linear vs exponential income** — time-trading is linear; offers, systems, teams, and IP are exponential vehicles.
5. **B.O.S.S. Moves (4 levels of business):** bake the offer → make offers → sell premium → scale with systems and teams. Business as ministry: the offer is how service reaches people at scale.

## Depth Layers

### Art
- The trash-man-to-cash-man arc: level 1 to level 4 in one biography; the map walked.
- Seed and harvest: the offer as seed, revenue as harvest, giving as the first planting.

### Wisdom
1. The 4 Levels resolve the pricing question structurally: price is set by the level the *offer* operates at, not the hours inside it.
2. Offer emission rate is the master metric preceding revenue.
3. Premium is an integrity position, not an extraction position, when the value gap is real (echoes STF Cost-Signal Fit and Brown's Two Poles).
4. Selling is transfer of certainty in service of the buyer's outcome.

### Practice
1. **Offer audit:** classify every current offer by the level it's priced at vs the level the work actually operates at; close the gap upward.
2. **Offer cadence:** a fixed rhythm of offers made per day/week, tracked like a vital sign.
3. **Premium anchor:** always have one offer at a price that requires the buyer's full commitment.
4. **Move up one level deliberately:** package implementation as communication, communication as imagination (method → IP → vehicle).
