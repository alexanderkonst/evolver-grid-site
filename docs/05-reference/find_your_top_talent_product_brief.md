# Find Your Top Talent

## Product Brief

**A self-knowledge and AI matching platform for high-agency communities.**

Find Your Top Talent helps communities move from talent density to real opportunities. Members clarify the role they are naturally here to play, what they are moving toward, and what resources, skills, and access they already hold. AI then surfaces possible collaboration matches so the community can see who should build, trade, refer, support, hire, or explore something real together.

## The Problem

Most communities are still directories of high-agency people, not coordination systems.

They gather impressive members, host events, run group chats, and create warm social fields. But the deeper value often remains invisible: who should collaborate with whom, why the match matters, and what concrete opportunity could emerge.

The hidden cost is low collaboration throughput. Members may enjoy the community but leave without projects, referrals, clients, collaborators, hires, trades, or venture seeds that would not have happened otherwise.

The core question for an ecosystem leader is:

**Can members point to something real that happened because they were in this community?**

---

## The Product

Find Your Top Talent turns self-knowledge into coordination infrastructure.

The first deployable unit is the **Community Member Onboarding Module**, built from three integrated technologies:

1. **Precision Purpose Discovery**  
   Identifies what role each member is naturally here to play.

2. **Precision Mission Discovery**  
   Clarifies what each member is moving toward.

3. **Precision Resource Mapping**  
   Maps what each member already has to build with: skills, assets, knowledge, networks, offers, access, and lived experience.

Once this data is visible and structured, AI can surface possible collaboration opportunities across the group. The product does not claim to force collaboration. It makes hidden collaboration opportunities visible, testable, and easier to act on.

---

## The Activation Format

The most direct front-door format is a **Collaboration Discovery Session**.

1. 5-10 volunteer members complete onboarding.
2. AI prepares pairings or small-group collaboration hypotheses.
3. Sasha hosts a live session with the community.
4. Members review the suggested matches together.
5. Each match is rated: does this make sense, would you explore it, what would make it more useful?
6. The leader receives a first map of hidden collaboration, venture, and project opportunities.

This collapses product demo, member value, market research, and proof into one event. The leader does not have to believe the theory. They can watch whether hidden opportunities become visible inside their own community.

## Architecture

The platform is built as a forkable, white-label coordination substrate.

**Member layer**
- Top Talent discovery
- Mission discovery
- Resource and asset mapping
- Quality of Life signal
- Collaboration readiness

**Community layer**
- Member onboarding
- AI matching
- Collaboration Discovery Sessions
- Community-specific skins and branding
- Optional cross-community matching

**Network layer**
- White-label deployments
- Opt-in data sharing for AI matching across communities
- Branded self-knowledge products and programs
- Revenue share model for digital products
- Steward network for governance, data protection, and decentralization

The design principle is simple: each community keeps its identity, while the shared engine improves as more communities use it.

---

## Business Model

The commercial model has four natural steps:

1. **Pilot session**  
   Run one Collaboration Discovery Session for a small member group.

2. **White-label deployment**  
   Run the onboarding and matching system under the community's brand.

3. **Branded self-knowledge products**  
   Communities offer built-in high-transformative-value tools and programs under their own brand, keeping 90 percent of digital-product revenue while 10 percent flows back to the shared engine.

4. **Stewardship and co-development**  
   Aligned communities can help evolve the platform, decentralize governance, and protect shared data infrastructure.

The key metric is **collaboration rate**: the share of members who find a collaborator and begin building, trading, referring, hiring, supporting, or exploring something concrete.
