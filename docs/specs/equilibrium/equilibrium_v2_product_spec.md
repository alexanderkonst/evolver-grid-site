## INPUT (5/5)

### 1. ICP (Who exactly?)

**Alexander himself** ‚Äî and founders like him who:
- Do 2-4 deep work sprints per day (writing, coding, strategizing)
- Already know Pomodoro is arbitrary and want something that matches their biology
- Use an iPad or second screen as a "work companion"
- Are curious about energy cycles but have no practical tool for it

**Not for:** People who want a task manager, calendar, or meditation app. This is a **work rhythm clock**, not a productivity tracker.

### 2. Transformation (Point A ‚Üí Point B)

**Point A:** "I sit down to work and look up 4 hours later, exhausted. I have no rhythm. I push through or drift. I know there must be a better way but Pomodoro timers feel dumb and arbitrary."

**Point B:** "I glance at Equilibrium and instantly know: I'm 12 minutes into my focus phase, this is sprint 2 of 3 today. The clock shows my pace. I don't need willpower to take breaks ‚Äî the rhythm carries me."

**One sentence:** *"Equilibrium shows you exactly where you are in your work rhythm so you can focus without thinking about when to stop."*

### 3. Pain of Point A

- "I look up after 4 hours and I'm drained ‚Äî no idea where the time went"
- "I skip breaks and then crash hard at 2pm"
- "Pomodoro beeps at 25 minutes and kills my flow"  
- "I feel like I should be taking breaks but I have no structure for it"
- "I start strong but by afternoon I have nothing left"

### 4. Dream Outcome

- "I glance at it and know: 8 minutes of focus left, then a natural pause"
- "I actually take breaks because the rhythm makes them feel obvious, not lazy"
- "I do 3 focused sprints and feel energized, not drained"
- "It's like having a conductor ‚Äî I just follow the beat"

### 5. Action (What user does)

Open Equilibrium on iPad or second screen. The breathing circle centers you. You tap "Enter Deep Focus" to begin a sprint. The clock shows you where you are in the 96-minute work rhythm ‚Äî 4 phases of 24 minutes each. At each transition, it gently suggests breathing or reflection. You don't manage ‚Äî you just follow. After 96 minutes, you rest.
