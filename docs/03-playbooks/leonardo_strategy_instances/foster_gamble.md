## Lessons (so far) — feed back to master playbook

This instance was the workshop where multiple integrity gates were *almost* violated and then caught. Worth documenting honestly:

1. **The artifact-iteration trap.** Across ~7 turns we produced 5 versions of the cancellation message, each more polished, with positive feedback reinforcing more polish. Polish is not the same as strategy. Caught and named.

2. **The channel trap.** The cancellation form is a low-protocol channel pre-framed as "exit." We optimized message-language for hours before questioning whether the channel itself was the right vehicle. Lesson: *the channel is part of the message; question it before optimizing inside it.*

3. **The cosmic word-salad trap.** Mid-iteration, an over-correction toward "extending Foster's cosmological frame" produced incoherent compressed mysticism (*"the Code patterns coordination, too; same toroidal geometry between people not just within them"*). Lesson: **when the patron's cosmology is your warming ground, let the artifact (the drawings) carry the resonance; do not try to perform native fluency in compressed jargon.** Plain English about what the prototype does; let the lyre do the deep work silently.

4. **The peer-claim trap.** "Peer-to-peer, not membership" was over-reach — claimed peer status on first contact before earning it. Caught. Removed. Sovereignty is *embodied*, not *declared*.

5. **The displacement-from-Si-Do trap.** This entire instance was workshopped while the active Si-Do gate (first $555 stranger from funnel unfired) remained untouched. Foster outreach was strategically real *and* a sideways move from the primary stuck point. The arc with Foster takes years; the funnel close takes weeks. Priority discipline matters.

6. **Listening to the founder's edits.** At one point Sasha's in-form edits were better than my regenerated versions, but I overrode them by regenerating from my own previous draft instead of seeing what he had typed. Lesson for AI partnership: *when the operator is editing in real time, show me what you have before I regenerate.*
