## The 5-Layer Pain Slicer

Pain is not monolithic. It has **layers**, and each layer reveals messaging language. Apply these sequentially â€” each goes deeper.

> *Applied correctly, this slicer produces the exact phrases your ICP uses to describe their situation â€” their self-language. These phrases become your messaging.*

### Layer 1: What They're Struggling With (Ğ‘Ğ¾Ñ€ÑŒĞ±Ğ°)

The **surface problem** they'd name if you asked them directly. This is what they'd tell a friend.

**Questions to uncover:**
- What would they say if you asked "what's going on?"
- What do they complain about?
- What do they Google at midnight?

**What to listen for beneath it:** What they WON'T say unprompted but feel deeply.

### Layer 2: Pressure (Ğ”Ğ°Ğ²Ğ»ĞµĞ½Ğ¸Ğµ)

What's **pushing them toward a decision**. External and internal forces creating a closing window.

**Questions to uncover:**
- What's making this urgent NOW (not "someday")?
- What external clocks are ticking? (financial runway, deadlines, market windows)
- What relationship dynamics are creating pressure?
- What self-image erosion is happening?

**Key insight:** Pressure compounds. Financial stress â†’ partner tension â†’ self-doubt â†’ decreased output â†’ slower progress â†’ more financial stress. Map the **loop**, not just the sources.

## The 5 Stages of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz Model)

| Stage | Name | Description | Example (Toothache) |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **1** | Unaware | Doesn't know they have a problem | Slight discomfort, ignored |
| **2** | Problem Aware | Knows the problem exists | "My tooth hurts, I admit it" |
| **3** | Solution Aware | Looking for solutions, doesn't know options | "What can I do about this?" |
| **4** | Product Aware | Knows different solutions exist | Dentist vs painkiller vs grandpa's vodka cotton |
| **5** | Most Aware | Comparing specific options, ready to buy | Looking at Google Maps for dentists |

At Stage 4, products from COMPLETELY DIFFERENT CATEGORIES compete:

## The Recursive Shadow of Pain

> *See [unique_gift_philosophy.md](../01-vision/unique_gift_philosophy.md) for full synthesis.*

Pain has a recursive dimension that standard pain mapping misses:

**A person's unique gift generates a limiting belief that is structurally identical to the gift, but inverted.**

| Gift | Shadow Belief | Pain Pattern |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Clarity | "I need more clarity before I can act" | Infinite preparation loop |
| Connection | "I feel isolated" | Withdrawal from the very relationships they'd enrich |
| Building | "Nothing I build is finished" | Starting new projects instead of shipping |
| Teaching | "I'm not qualified enough" | Over-studying instead of sharing |

**The practical implication**: When slicing pain (Layers 1-5), also ask: *"What is this person's unique gift?"* â€” then check if their pain is the shadow of that gift. If it is, the intervention is not more preparation. The intervention is **helping them see that the base case was met long ago** â€” they have enough to act.

**For the practitioner**: The recursive shadow also explains underpricing. If your gift is making the invisible visible, your shadow is: "my own value is invisible to me." This is not a character flaw â€” it is the structural property of recursion operating on the practitioner.
