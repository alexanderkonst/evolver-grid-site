# Part A: Distribution — Channels & Reach

## The Doctrine

- **Channels are downstream of the axiom.** Distribution strategy is not chosen from a menu — it emerges FROM the myth and the meta-position. When the axiom is clear, the right channels become obvious.
- **A channel is wherever people hit the ceiling and say so out loud.** The ceiling (see `docs/02-strategy/roadmap.md` and the Day 127 myth work) is the diagnostic; anywhere someone names their own limit is a live distribution surface.
- **Artifact-led distribution — the Leonardo pattern.** The artifact (the reveal, the reading, the blueprint) IS the distribution mechanism: it demonstrates the method by performing it, for free, on the prospect. Full pattern: [`leonardo_strategy_for_the_golden_age.md`](./leonardo_strategy_for_the_golden_age.md).
- **Every owned digital surface is a free billboard.** Map, score, and project the unique business architecture onto the highest-leverage surfaces before spending energy on new ones.

## 1. The School in One Move

The diagnosis IS the demo — you sell articulation by performing it on the prospect, free.

The five laws of Mirror Selling, verbatim from [`integral_transactional_field_theory.md`](../01-vision/integral_transactional_field_theory.md) Part X:

1. **Give all diagnosis, sell all derivation.** Naming is free (their pattern, their stuck, their cost, even a strong articulation seed); building is paid (the co-verified formulation, offer, pricing, page, product core). Sayable: «Диагностика бесплатно. Сборка за деньги.»
2. **Better diagnosis is better for both, always.** A brilliant free diagnosis is simultaneously the gift, the proof, and the appetite: clarity increases hunger for operationalization. You cannot lose by diagnosing too well.
3. **Catch buying questions; never answer them with explanations.** A buying question is the prospect stating the product as a question ('how do I sell lightness and energy?'). Point at it: 'what you just asked is exactly the work.'
4. **The call opens the gap; the reflection connects the dots; the session closes them.** Live calls are for eliciting their words (Elicit-Provide-Elicit); the same-day Reflection Proposal (ops §4a) mirrors their own words back with one door; closing happens when yes-energy appears: 'if you're ready, let's set it up right now' — their ask, your door, zero pressure.
5. **The platform is the naming machine.** The free high-precision reveal is the school's infrastructure: warm leads take the free assessment before the call (arriving with raw naming, so the call goes deeper), or receive it as the gift inside the reflection message. As naming gets better and cheaper at scale, whoever names best owns the front door to all building.

Pointer: full lineage (Mirror-Not-Teacher, Sales as Love, MI's Elicit-Provide-Elicit, SPIN, Challenger, Karime's proposal) lives in ITFT Part X.

## 2. The Direction Call Script

7 stages, 45 minutes, one emotional arc:

| Time | Stage | Emotion | Exact one-line |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-3 | Frame | Safe | "These 45 minutes are fully yours. At the end I'll name what I see and one next step, and you're free either way." |
| 3-15 | Their field | Seen | "What's the transition you're in? And what have you tried?" |
| 15-18 | The cost | Stirred | "What has all that trying cost you so far?" |
| 18-30 | The mirror | Struck | "Can I say back the one thread I hear under all of it?" |
| 30-40 | Empowerment | Free | "What are you seeing right now?" then "What would having that answer change for you?" |
| 40-45 | The doors | Free | Pitch paragraph + "Both doors are genuinely fine with me" — then silence. If yes-energy: "if you're ready, let's set it up right now" (logistics on the call). |
| Same evening | Gifted | — | The Reflection Proposal (pointer: `outreach_ops.md` §4a). |

**Note on stage 4 (the mirror):** the emotion is STRUCK — the oh-shit of seeing the gap clearly for the first time. Not relief. Relief belongs to the paid session, when the gap actually closes.

## 3. The Incepting Questions (per segment)

| Segment | Incepting question |
|---|---|
| Polymath | "What is the one thing all of these projects are doing?" |
| Practitioner | "What would your best client say you do that nobody else does?" |
| Founder between ventures | "Strip away the company. What were you actually building all along?" — caution: presumes connection. For founders with co-founders or unconnected ventures, open with "What are you searching for, and how will you know you found it?" instead. |
| Founder with live business | The tagline pair: "What's your tagline?" → "Would your best customer say that's what they actually bought?" |
| Employee | "What exactly is your next employer buying, beyond the title?" |
| Fractional | "What are all your gigs adding up to?" |
| Community leader | "Who in your community should be working together right now and doesn't know it?" |
| Investor | "What can you see in a founder that nobody else can price?" |
| Teen (RU) | «Чем ты отличаешься от всех, кто подаёт в тот же вуз с теми же баллами?» |

**Mechanics note:** each question asks them to articulate the invariant their stage depends on. The law guarantees they can't, yet. The question is the product wearing a question mark.

## 4. The Three Collapse Conditions

Attention, Pain, and Purchasing Power must all collapse together for a sale to happen — one-paragraph summary; full theory in [`integral_transactional_field_theory.md`](../01-vision/integral_transactional_field_theory.md) Part II.

- Stage 2 (their field) creates **Attention**.
- Stage 3 (the cost) creates **Pain**, spoken in their own voice, not asserted by you.
- Stage 6 (the doors) tests **Purchasing Power**.

## 7. Pre-Call Protocol

- Two questions asked in-channel after booking (unchanged): "What's the transition you're in? And what have you tried?"
- **Optional free assessment before the call**, framed as a gift, never required. Copy: "most people do it; the call goes deeper." (see `outreach_ops.md` §3.2 v2 snippet).
- **Warm ZoG alumni:** invite them to re-take — the current version is far stronger than what they saw before.
- **Never require full onboarding pre-call** — it adds friction.
- **Teens:** no pre-work.

The self-knowledge objection disarm — used when a prospect questions whether they can really see themselves clearly with help. Pointer: full bank in `outreach_ops.md` §6 (copy bank).

## 9. The Sent-Copy Corpus

Every final sent wording is archived verbatim in the CRM's Outreach Message Bank → Sent messages (private ledger), tagged scenario + outcome. That corpus is the calibration set for all future AI-drafted copy — new drafts get checked against what actually got sent and what actually converted. Full rule: `outreach_ops.md` §8.
