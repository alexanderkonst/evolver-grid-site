## Five Strategic Questions

### 1. Why Is Distribution Shaped This Way?

Distribution is shaped by **where attention already lives**.

The internet created infinite shelf space but finite attention.
Result: discovery became the bottleneck, not supply.

Traditional distribution was about **logistics** (getting physical product to location).
Modern distribution is about **accessibility of attention** (being present when decision happens).

The shape follows attention:
- Platforms own attention â†’ platform distribution dominates
- Communities hold trust â†’ community distribution converts better
- Algorithms control visibility â†’ paid/organic game becomes essential

> The shape of distribution = the shape of attention infrastructure.

### 2. What's Great About Distribution?

**Leverage.** Once built, distribution compounds while you sleep.

| What's Great | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| **Scalability** | One channel can reach millions |
| **Compounding** | Reputation builds over time |
| **Automation** | Systems work without manual effort |
| **Network effects** | Users distribute for you |
| **Measurability** | Every click, conversion is trackable |

Best case: product distributes itself (virality).
Second best: systems distribute for you (automation).
Worst case: you distribute manually (burnout).

### 3. What Sucks About Distribution?

**Platform dependency and fragility.**

| What Sucks | Reality |
|------------|---------|
| **Platform risk** | Algorithm change = traffic gone |
| **Gatekeepers** | Marketplaces take 30%, control visibility |
| **Noise** | Infinite competition for finite attention |
| **Cold start problem** | Zero users = zero distribution |
| **Maintenance** | Channels require constant feeding |

The hidden cost: distribution requires ONGOING energy.
It's not "build once, done forever."
It's "build once, maintain continuously."

### 4. The Biggest Problem to Solve?

**Cold start + channel-product fit.**

> How do you get the first 100 users when you have zero distribution?

The paradox:
- Distribution requires users (social proof, virality)
- Users require distribution (discovery)

**Solutions:**
1. **Borrow distribution** â€” partner with someone who has it
2. **Guerrilla tactics** â€” physical presence where ICP gathers
3. **Content flywheel** â€” create value that attracts attention
4. **Direct outreach** â€” 1:1 until you have momentum
5. **Product-led** â€” make the product spread itself

The second problem: **channel-product fit**.
Not every product works on every channel.
Coaching â‰  TikTok. SaaS â‰  Instagram. B2B â‰  Facebook.

### 5. How Do You Win in This Model?

**Own your distribution. Minimize platform dependency.**

| Winning Strategy | How |
|------------------|-----|
| **Build owned channels** | Email list, community, direct relationships |
| **Create virality** | Product output is shareable |
| **Become the platform** | Multi-sided marketplace where users bring users |
| **Compound reputation** | Consistent delivery â†’ trust â†’ referrals |
| **Multi-channel resilience** | Never depend on one channel |

> The winner owns the relationship with the customer, not the platform.

**The Ultimate Win:**
Product that distributes itself + owned channels + reputation = distribution moat.

## Distribution Principles

### Principle 1: Go Where They Already Are

> Don't ask people to come to you. Be where they already gather.

**Application:**
- What communities does your ICP inhabit?
- What rituals do they already perform?
- What platforms do they already use?

### Principle 2: Minimize Friction to Zero

> Every click, every form field, every delay = lost transaction.

**Application:**
- One-click purchase where possible
- No unnecessary registration
- Instant access / delivery

### Principle 3: Embed Distribution in Product

> The best distribution is invisible â€” it happens because the product spreads itself.

**Application:**
- Shareable outputs (infographics, reports, profiles)
- Referral incentives
- "Powered by X" attribution
- Network effects

### Principle 4: Match Channel to ICP Stage

> Different channels work for different awareness stages.

| Awareness Stage | Best Channels |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Unaware | Content, passive visibility |
| Problem Aware | Communities, search |
| Solution Aware | Comparison sites, reviews |
| Product Aware | Direct, retargeting |
| Most Aware | Email, DM, 1:1 |

### Principle 5: Distribution Is Reputation Over Time

> Every successful transaction = trust deposited.

The channel matters less than the pattern.
Consistent, clean delivery builds reputation.
Reputation = distribution capital.
